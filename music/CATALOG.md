## Variation Types Explained

### Category 1: Harmonic Elaborations (Variations 1-3)

Each emblem gets three different harmonic voicings of the original melody:

1. **Root Position Harmony** — Add thirds and fifths below the melody for classical harmony
2. **First Inversion Voicing** — Reposition voices for flowing counterpoint
3. **Close Voicing** — Keep all voices within a single octave for compact texture

**Best for:** Listening to the harmonic richness of Maier's original canons; understanding the intervallic relationships.

### Category 2: Scale Runs & Improvisations (Variations 4-6)

Each long note gets filled with scalar passages and arpeggios, in the style of Renaissance improvisation:

4. **Diatonic Scalar Runs** — Fill intervals with stepwise motion in C major
5. **Chromatic Approach Notes** — Add chromatic leading tones and passing tones
6. **Arpeggio Fills** — Replace long notes with broken-chord figures

**Best for:** Hearing improvisation technique; appreciating rhythmic elaboration; background study music.

### Category 3: Digital Effects (Variations 7-10)

Each original melody is processed with digital audio effects, transforming it into ambient/experimental territory:

7. **Cathedral Reverb** (Wet: 70%, Decay: 2.5s) — Spacious ecclesiastical reverb, like singing in a cathedral
8. **Vibrato Wobble** (Depth: 15%, Rate: 5.5 Hz) — Sine LFO modulation + tremolo, creates wobbling NES effect
9. **Echo Cascade** (Wet: 60%, Triplet Delays) — Multi-tap delays at 375/750/1125ms with feedback
10. **Spacious Chorus** (Combined) — Gentle reverb + vibrato + delay, most experimental

**Best for:** Atmosphere; meditation; experimental listening; unique interpretations of alchemical themes.
